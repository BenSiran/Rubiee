Introduction

Welcome to this comprehensive IELTS Speaking preparation guide. This resource covers all three parts of the IELTS Speaking test with sample questions, model answers, and practical exercises to help you achieve your target band score.

The IELTS Speaking test assesses your ability to communicate effectively in English through a face-to-face interview with an examiner. It lasts 11-14 minutes and is divided into three distinct parts.

PART 1: Introduction and Interview (4-5 minutes)

In Part 1, the examiner asks general questions about yourself and familiar topics such as home, family, work, studies, and interests.

üè† SET 1: Home and Accommodation

QuestionsModel Answers
1. Do you live in a house or an apartment?"I currently live in an apartment in the city center. It's a two-bedroom flat on the fifth floor of a modern building, which I share with a roommate."
2. What do you like most about where you live?"What I appreciate most is the location. It's extremely convenient because I'm within walking distance of shops, restaurants, and public transportation. The neighborhood is also quite safe and has a friendly atmosphere."
3. How long have you lived there?"I've been living there for about two years now, ever since I started my university studies. Time has really flown by, and I've grown quite comfortable with the area."
4. Would you like to move to a different place in the future?"Yes, eventually I would. While I enjoy the convenience of city living, I'd prefer to move to a quieter suburban area in the future, perhaps somewhere with a small garden. I think it would be better for raising a family."

PART 2: Long Turn (3-4 minutes)

In Part 2, you receive a task card with a topic and prompts. You have 1 minute to prepare and should speak for 1-2 minutes.

üåç Topic Card 1: Describe a memorable journey

Task Card:

Describe a memorable journey you have taken.

You should say:

  • Where you went
  • Who you went with
  • What you did there
  • And explain why this journey was memorable

"I'd like to talk about a trip I took to Japan about two years ago, which was truly unforgettable. I went with three close friends from university during our summer break, and we spent two weeks exploring the country... What made this journey particularly memorable was that it was my first time traveling abroad without my family... I was also fascinated by how the Japanese people managed to preserve their traditional customs while embracing modern technology... Perhaps most importantly, this trip strengthened my friendships. These shared experiences and the memories we created together have made this journey stand out as one of the best experiences of my life."

üí° Topic Card 2: Describe a skill you would like to learn

Task Card:

Describe a skill you would like to learn.

You should say:

  • What the skill is
  • How you would learn it
  • Why you want to learn it
  • And explain what difference it would make to your life

"I'd really like to learn how to play the piano, which is something I've been considering for quite some time now... If I were to learn this skill, I'd probably start by taking formal lessons from a qualified instructor... I also believe that learning an instrument would be intellectually stimulating and help develop discipline and patience... Learning to play the piano would make a significant difference to my daily routine and overall wellbeing. It would give me a creative outlet to express emotions and relieve stress after a demanding day... I see it as an investment in lifelong learning and personal growth that would continue to bring joy for years to come."

üë§ Topic Card 3: Describe a person who has influenced you

You should say:

  • Who this person is
  • How you know them

I often think about her advice when I face difficult situations: to be persistent, to believe in myself, and to always keep learning."

PART 3: Two-Way Discussion (4-5 minutes)

Part 3 involves more abstract questions related to the Part 2 topic. The examiner explores ideas and issues in greater depth.

‚úàÔ∏è Discussion 1: Travel and Tourism

QuestionsModel Answers (Key Points)
**Q1: How has tourism changed over the past few decades?**Accessibility (budget airlines); Technology (online booking, social media/Instagram tourism); Challenges (over-tourism, shift to sustainable tourism).
**Q2: What are the advantages and disadvantages of international travel?****Pros:** Broadens perspectives, builds confidence, creates economic opportunities. **Cons:** Carbon footprint, damage to ecosystems, commodification of local cultures, economic inequality.
**Q3: Do you think virtual reality could replace actual travel in the future?**No, but will complement it. VR lacks engagement of all five senses (smell, taste, touch, spontaneity). Real travel offers personal growth and unplanned discoveries. VR is great for previewing or inaccessible places.

üìö Discussion 2: Education and Learning

QuestionsModel Answers (Key Points)
**Q1: How important is formal education in today's society?**Provides essential skills (literacy, critical thinking). Offers professional credentials/gatekeepers. Facilitates networking. Role is evolving due to high costs and rise of alternatives (online platforms).
**Q2: What makes a good teacher?**Deep subject knowledge and passion. Pedagogical skills (clear communication, varied methods). Interpersonal skills (patience, empathy, rapport). Mentorship and setting high expectations. Continuous reflection/improvement.
**Q3: Should education focus more on practical skills or theoretical knowledge?**Integration is key (false dichotomy). Theory provides foundational understanding for adaptation. Practical skills prepare for the real world (employment). Balance varies by field (e.g., vocational vs. research).

ü§ñ Discussion 3: Technology and Society

QuestionsModel Answers (Key Points)
**Q1: How has technology changed the way people communicate?**Increased speed and reach (global connection). Altered quality (lack of non-verbal cues, misunderstandings, 'keyboard courage'). Constant connectivity blurs boundaries (anxiety). Paradox: more connected, yet potentially lonelier.
**Q2: Do the benefits of artificial intelligence outweigh the risks?**Benefits are substantial (healthcare diagnostics, research). Risks are equally significant (job displacement, algorithmic bias, privacy). Outcome depends on regulation and ethical development. Proactive policy is required.
**Q3: How might technology affect employment in the future?**Elimination of routine jobs (automation/AI). Creation of new job categories (AI ethicists, app developers). Shift to uniquely human skills (creativity, emotional intelligence). Policy needed to manage transition (UBI, retraining).

PRACTICAL EXERCISES

Exercise 1: Part 1 Response Development

Choose one question from each set below. Record yourself answering each question. Aim for 30-45 seconds per response. Then, listen to your recording and evaluate your response.

  • **Set A: Daily Routine** (e.g., What time do you usually wake up?)
  • **Set B: Food** (e.g., What kinds of food are popular in your country?)
  • **Set C: Weather** (e.g., What's the weather like in your hometown?)

Exercise 2: Part 2 Practice Cards

Choose one card below. Set a timer for 1 minute to prepare notes. Then speak for 2 minutes on the topic. Record yourself and evaluate your coverage and timing.

  • **Practice Card A:** Describe a book that had an impact on you.
  • **Practice Card B:** Describe a time when you helped someone.
  • **Practice Card C:** Describe an important decision you made.

Exercise 3: Part 3 Critical Thinking

Practice answering each question for 60-90 seconds. Focus on presenting multiple perspectives and using linking words.

  • **Topic 1:** Environment (e.g., Can economic development and environmental protection coexist?)
  • **Topic 2:** Social Media (e.g., Is social media making people more connected or more isolated?)
  • **Topic 3:** Globalization (e.g., How does globalization affect local cultures?)

Exercise 4: Vocabulary Building

For each theme, create a mind map with at least 15 related words/phrases. Write three sample sentences using vocabulary from each theme.

Exercise 5: Fluency and Coherence

Practice using **Discourse Markers** (e.g., Initially, Conversely, Furthermore, Consequently) naturally in your responses.

Exercise 6: Pronunciation Practice

Record yourself reading sample sentences, focusing on Word Stress and Intonation.

Exercise 7: Mock Test Simulation

Complete a full mock speaking test (Part 1, 2, and 3) and evaluate against the four main criteria.

Exercise 8: Self-Assessment Checklist

Use the checklist provided in the guide after each practice session to track your improvements in Fluency, Vocabulary, Grammar, and Pronunciation.

Final Tips from Instructor Ben Siran

  1. Practice Regularly: Consistency is key.
  2. Record Yourself: Essential for self-correction.
  3. Expand Your Answers: Use the PREP method (Point, Reason, Example, Personal experience).
  4. Stay Calm: Ask for clarification if needed.
  5. Be Yourself: Speak honestly and naturally.
  6. Think in English: Avoid translating.
  7. Use Signposting Language: Guide the examiner through your thoughts.
  8. Manage Your Time: Utilize the full 2 minutes in Part 2.
  9. Show Range: Demonstrate varied vocabulary and complex structures.
  10. Stay Positive: Approach the test as an opportunity.
